# Ozvor — Competitor Battle Cards

> Owner: sales-researcher · Last updated: 2026-07-02
> Scope: Peec AI, Profound, Otterly.AI, AthenaHQ, Semrush AI Toolkit / Ahrefs Brand Radar
> All pricing/feature claims below are sourced inline. Where a primary pricing page did not render a dollar figure, secondary sources (review aggregators, G2) are cited and flagged as such — not fabricated.

## TL;DR

All five competitors are monitoring-first tools: they measure AI-search visibility (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, Google AI Overviews) but stop at diagnosis — none write content, and none offer a done-for-you execution arm. Entry pricing ranges from Otterly's $29/mo to AthenaHQ's $295/mo and Semrush/Ahrefs' $99-$828/mo bolt-ons; Profound and AthenaHQ gate real functionality behind $295-$399+/mo tiers. Ozvor's edge: full loop (audit → evidence-backed plan → BYOK content drafts → OrganicPosts DFY), radical measurement honesty (public methodology, audits that fail honestly instead of fabricating data), and SMB/agency pricing ($0 → $29 → $99 → $549 white-label). Where we lose: enterprise buyers needing SSO/SOC2, 10+ engines, or an incumbent SEO suite they already pay for. Primary objection: "another dashboard with no action" — countered by showing the plan + content draft generated in the same session as the audit.

## 1. Peec AI (peec.ai)

**What they do**: AI search analytics platform for marketing teams and SEO agencies. Tracks visibility, position, and sentiment across AI engines, benchmarks against competitors, and surfaces which sources AI models cite. No content generation — diagnosis only. [peec.ai](https://peec.ai) [peec.ai/pricing](https://peec.ai/pricing)

**Pricing** (per secondary sources — Peec's own pricing page did not render dollar figures on fetch, so treat as corroborated-but-verify): Starter ~$95/mo (50 prompts, 3 models), Pro ~$245/mo (150 prompts), Advanced ~$495/mo (350 prompts); additional AI engines beyond the included 3 cost $35-$165/mo each; 15% off annual; 7-day free trial. Enterprise custom. Sources: [Geoptie review](https://geoptie.com/blog/peec-ai-review), [workduo.ai](https://www.workduo.ai/blog/peec-ai-pricing), [peec.ai/pricing](https://peec.ai/pricing) (page confirmed to exist, exact figures unverified on direct fetch — **flag: pricing figures below $95/$245/$495 are review-site-sourced, not Peec's own page text**).

**Engines covered**: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini as base 3; AI Mode, Microsoft Copilot, AI Overviews available; Enterprise adds Qwen, DeepSeek, Claude Sonnet 4, GPT-5 Search via API. [peec.ai](https://peec.ai)

**Strengths**: 5.0/5 across 9 verified G2 reviews as of May 2026 — users praise intuitive UI, support, and tying AI visibility to real outcomes. [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/peec-ai/reviews) Claims to query AI platforms by simulating real browser sessions rather than API calls, positioned as avoiding accuracy gaps of API-only competitors. [G2 review summary](https://www.g2.com/products/peec-ai/reviews) Fast-funded and fast-growing (per discovery.md competitive landscape, flagged there as partially unverified).

**Weaknesses/gaps vs Ozvor**: Explicitly diagnosis-only — "tracks mentions but doesn't write content, build authority signals, or implement technical optimizations." [G2 review summary](https://www.g2.com/products/peec-ai/reviews) No action plan generator, no BYOK content engine, no DFY arm. Per-prompt pricing scales fast (additional engines cost $35-165/mo each). No public methodology page found confirming measurement approach in detail beyond marketing copy.

**How we win**: Peec tells you where you stand; Ozvor tells you where you stand AND hands you an evidence-backed action plan plus ready-to-review content drafts in the same session — no separate content tool, no agency handoff required to act on the data. Our BYOK model means the client controls the LLM cost for content generation, which Peec has no equivalent for since it doesn't generate content at all. For a solo founder or small agency, our $29 Get-Cited Kit and $99/mo Growth tier undercut Peec's $95-245/mo entry points while adding the plan/content layer Peec lacks.

**When we lose**: If a prospect only wants monitoring — no interest in acting on findings themselves and already has a content team — and needs deep multi-engine coverage (6+ models) with a polished agency-grade UI today, Peec's dedicated monitoring depth and 5.0 G2 rating make it a legitimate choice.

**Landmine question**: "When Peec tells you your citation rate dropped, what happens next in your workflow — who writes the content to fix it, and how long does that take?"

## 2. Profound (tryprofound.com)

**What they do**: Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) platform for enterprise marketing teams — tracks brand visibility across AI answer engines, provides "Agent" credits for AI-assisted analysis, and an "Opportunities" panel. Enterprise-grade reporting and API access. [tryprofound.com](https://www.tryprofound.com) [tryprofound.com/pricing](https://www.tryprofound.com/pricing)

**Pricing**: Starter $99/mo billed yearly (ChatGPT only, 50 prompts/mo, 100 Agent credits, 1 seat); Growth $399/mo billed yearly (3 answer engines, 100 prompts/mo, 400 Agent credits, 3 seats); Enterprise custom (up to 10 engines, SSO/SAML, SOC2, multi-company). [tryprofound.com/pricing](https://www.tryprofound.com/pricing) Independent review corroborates: "$99/mo Starter is enough to see what Profound does—but not enough to run a meaningful AEO program... real functionality starts at $399/month (Growth)." [Cairrot](https://cairrot.com/alternatives/profound-review-price-comparison-top-alternatives/)

**Engines covered**: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot, Meta AI, Grok, DeepSeek, Anthropic Claude (up to 10 total on Enterprise). [tryprofound.com](https://www.tryprofound.com)

**Strengths**: 4.6/5 across 845 G2 reviews — strong marks for analytics depth, customer support, and reporting value. [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/profound/reviews) SOC2 and SSO/SAML on Enterprise — genuinely enterprise-ready. Broadest engine list of the group when fully unlocked (10).

**Weaknesses/gaps vs Ozvor**: Recurring G2 complaint is a steep learning curve and "confusing" dashboard without dedicated onboarding, which is Enterprise-only. [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/profound/reviews) Starter tier is functionally a teaser (1 engine, 50 prompts) — real usage requires $399/mo minimum. No content generation beyond "Agent credits" (scope/output not independently verified — treat as unverified). No SMB-priced path; no white-label agency tier at Ozvor's price point; no DFY arm.

**How we win**: Profound's own reviewers say the useful tier starts at $399/mo and still requires someone dedicated to running the dashboard. Ozvor's $99/mo Growth tier ships an audit, a plan, and content drafts together — no $399/mo floor, no dedicated ops hire needed to interpret it. For agencies, our $549/mo Agency tier (15 brands, white-label, about $37 per brand) is priced for a multi-client retainer business, not a single enterprise brand.

**When we lose**: A Fortune 1000 or venture-backed company that needs SOC2, SSO, 10-engine coverage, and has budget for a $399-2,000+/mo tool with a dedicated marketing ops person to run it should choose Profound — that's a genuinely different buyer than ours.

**Landmine question**: "At $99/mo Starter you get ChatGPT only and 50 prompts — is that enough to actually run a program, or were you already planning to budget for the $399 tier?"

## 3. Otterly.AI (otterly.ai)

**What they do**: Mid-market AI search monitoring — prompt tracking across engines with full response capture, citation/domain-trust analysis, and GEO audits that grade pages on AI-readiness factors (content depth, structure). [otterly.ai](https://otterly.ai) — pricing page fetch failed (connection error); pricing corroborated via search aggregation below.

**Pricing**: Lite $29/mo (15 prompts, 1,000 audits); Standard $189/mo (100 prompts, 5,000 audits, 150 for Agency Partners); Premium $489/mo (400 prompts, 10,000 audits, 500 for Agency Partners); 15% off annual; Gemini and Google AI Mode are paid add-ons ($9-149/mo each). Sources: [thatmarketingbuddy.com](https://thatmarketingbuddy.com/pricing/otterly-ai), [tryanalyze.ai review](https://www.tryanalyze.ai/blog/otterly-ai-review). This matches discovery.md's prior figures (§3), so treated as corroborated across two independent passes.

**Engines covered**: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Microsoft Copilot as base four; Gemini and Google AI Mode require paid add-ons. [tryanalyze.ai](https://www.tryanalyze.ai/blog/otterly-ai-review)

**Strengths**: Lowest published entry price in the direct-monitoring category ($29/mo) — genuinely SMB-accessible on paper. 50+ country tracking on every tier. GEO audit feature (page-level AI-readiness grading) is a real differentiator among pure monitoring tools.

**Weaknesses/gaps vs Ozvor**: "No content creation tools. It audits existing pages but cannot write or optimize content... no automation capabilities for turning insights into action." [tryanalyze.ai](https://www.tryanalyze.ai/blog/otterly-ai-review) No GA4 integration, no traffic/revenue attribution — "you see visibility but never learn if it drives conversions." [tryanalyze.ai](https://www.tryanalyze.ai/blog/otterly-ai-review) Reported data-refresh lag from scheduled crawl cycles. Per-prompt pricing scales expensively — "$189/month plan covers only 100 prompts, and add-ons push costs to $300+." [tryanalyze.ai](https://www.tryanalyze.ai/blog/otterly-ai-review)

**How we win**: Otterly's own review coverage confirms it has zero content generation and zero action automation — it is pure diagnosis, same gap as Peec. Our $29 Get-Cited Kit matches Otterly's Lite price point but includes an evidence-backed action plan, not just a score. As usage grows, Otterly's per-prompt pricing model compounds toward $300+/mo for what our $99/mo Growth tier covers with content drafts included.

**When we lose**: A team that specifically wants granular per-page GEO audits (content depth/structure grading) as a standalone SEO-adjacent workflow, and is comfortable pairing it with their own separate content and dev resources, may prefer Otterly's narrower, cheaper entry tier.

**Landmine question**: "If Otterly's audit flags a page as AI-unreadable, who on your team writes the fix — and does anyone track whether it actually moved your citation rate afterward?"

## 4. AthenaHQ (athenahq.ai)

**What they do**: "Agents to win on AI search" — tracks and analyzes brand visibility across AI engines and includes an on-page/off-page "content optimization agent" that takes actions, plus a Citation Engine and Knowledge Base on higher tiers. Positions itself as more action-oriented than pure monitoring tools. [athenahq.ai](https://athenahq.ai) [athenahq.ai/plans](https://athenahq.ai/plans)

**Pricing**: Essential (free) — $25 free credit / 300 credits, basic prompt/response analysis, competitor insights. Starter $295/mo — $300/mo credit (3,600 credits), 9 AI models, API access, content optimization agent, on-page/off-page actions. Enterprise custom — custom credits, Knowledge Base, Oracle discrepancy detection, Athena Citation Engine, SSO/SAML, audit logs, white-glove setup. 17% off annual. Promotions sometimes discount first month to ~$95. [athenahq.ai/plans](https://athenahq.ai/plans) **Note**: discovery.md (§3) cites AthenaHQ Starter at $95/mo — that figure is now stale; current verified Starter price is $295/mo as of this research pass ([athenahq.ai/plans](https://athenahq.ai/plans), corroborated by [search aggregation](https://trakkr.ai/reviews)). Flagging the discrepancy rather than silently reconciling it.

**Engines covered**: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Google AI Mode, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, Grok, plus more on request (9 models on Starter). [athenahq.ai/plans](https://athenahq.ai/plans)

**Strengths**: Genuinely more action-oriented than Peec/Otterly — the "content optimization agent" and on-page/off-page actions are a real step toward closing the loop, not just diagnosis. Free tier is usable for a first look. Broad engine coverage (9 models) at the Starter tier, more than Profound's Starter.

**Weaknesses/gaps vs Ozvor**: $295/mo Starter is a 3x-to-10x jump over our entry tiers ($29 Kit / $99 Growth) for comparable core functionality. Credit-based pricing (1 credit = 1 AI response) adds a metering layer that can create usage anxiety at scale — not independently verified how fast 3,600 credits/mo are consumed at typical audit cadence. No BYOK model found — content-agent cost model not disclosed publicly, so client cost control is unverified. No white-label agency tier or DFY arm found on public pages.

**How we win**: AthenaHQ validates our "full loop" thesis — a monitoring-only tool isn't enough, which is exactly why they built an action agent too. But their Starter tier costs $295/mo, roughly 3x our Growth tier, before you reach anything with real optimization actions. Our BYOK content model means the client sees and controls the exact model/cost used to generate content, versus AthenaHQ's opaque credit-metering system.

**When we lose**: A team with budget for $295+/mo that wants an all-in-one "agent" doing on-page and off-page actions automatically, and values Oracle discrepancy detection / Knowledge Base features (Enterprise-only), may prefer AthenaHQ's more automated action layer over our human-approved BYOK drafting model — especially if they don't need a done-for-you human execution arm.

**Landmine question**: "At $295/mo for 3,600 credits, what happens to your program the month you burn through them faster than expected?"

## 5. Semrush AI Toolkit / Ahrefs Brand Radar — "the incumbent adds an AI module" play

**What they do**: Both are AI-visibility bolt-ons sold by established SEO suites, not purpose-built GEO platforms. Semrush AI Visibility Toolkit tracks brand mentions/prompts across AI engines as an add-on to (or bundled with) the existing Semrush SEO product. [semrush.com/kb/1493-ai-visibility-toolkit](https://www.semrush.com/kb/1493-ai-visibility-toolkit) Ahrefs Brand Radar tracks brand visibility across AI answers using a database of 400M+ search-backed prompts, sold as an add-on requiring an active Ahrefs base subscription. [help.ahrefs.com](https://help.ahrefs.com/en/articles/11064852-what-is-brand-radar-and-how-to-use-it)

**Pricing**: Semrush AI Visibility Toolkit — $99/mo per domain standalone (25 custom prompts); or bundled into "Semrush One": Starter $199/mo (AI visibility across 5+ platforms, 50 prompts, 500 keywords), Pro+ $299/mo, Advanced $549/mo. No free trial for the standalone AI toolkit. Sources: [demandsage.com](https://www.demandsage.com/semrush-pricing/), [aiproductivity.ai](https://aiproductivity.ai/blog/semrush-pricing/) (secondary sources; Semrush's own pricing page did not render dollar figures on direct fetch — **flag as review-site corroborated**). Ahrefs Brand Radar — requires base Ahrefs plan (from $129/mo Lite+); single-platform AI Index add-on $199/mo, all-6-platforms bundle $699/mo; realistic full coverage cost ~$828/mo combined. Custom-prompt add-on tiers: Basic $50/mo (2,500 checks), Growth $100/mo (7,000 checks), Scale $250/mo (25,000 checks). [help.ahrefs.com](https://help.ahrefs.com/en/articles/11064852-what-is-brand-radar-and-how-to-use-it), corroborated by [tryanalyze.ai](https://www.tryanalyze.ai/blog/ahrefs-brand-radar-review).

**Engines covered**: Semrush — 5+ platforms per bundled tier (exact list not confirmed on fetch; treat platform count as unverified beyond "5+"). Ahrefs Brand Radar — Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, Grok (6 engines); notably missing Claude. [help.ahrefs.com](https://help.ahrefs.com/en/articles/11064852-what-is-brand-radar-and-how-to-use-it)

**Strengths**: Both benefit from being bundled with tools SMBs and agencies already pay for and trust for traditional SEO — zero net-new vendor relationship for existing Semrush/Ahrefs customers. Ahrefs Brand Radar's prompt database (400M+ search-backed prompts) reflects real user search behavior rather than synthetic prompt libraries for the AI Overviews signal specifically. [help.ahrefs.com](https://help.ahrefs.com/en/articles/11064852-what-is-brand-radar-and-how-to-use-it)

**Weaknesses/gaps vs Ozvor**: Real accuracy concerns reported: independent testing found Ahrefs Brand Radar reported only 3 ChatGPT mentions where manual verification found 123, and 6 Perplexity mentions vs. 212 actual — a significant undercount gap. [tryanalyze.ai](https://www.tryanalyze.ai/blog/ahrefs-brand-radar-review) (Note: this specific test was run by a competing vendor's content, so treat the exact numbers as a claim requiring independent re-verification, not an established fact — but the direction, that bolt-on AI modules undercount relative to purpose-built tools, is consistent with the "retrofitted" critique noted in discovery.md.) Claude is missing from Ahrefs' engine list entirely. Full multi-platform coverage on either tool requires stacking add-ons on top of a base subscription, pushing realistic cost to $199-828+/mo. Neither offers content generation, an evidence-backed action plan, or a DFY arm — they are measurement modules retrofitted onto SEO platforms, not GEO-native products.

**How we win**: These are SEO tools with an AI module bolted on, not GEO-native platforms — the accuracy gap reported for Ahrefs (missing the large majority of actual ChatGPT/Perplexity mentions in one independent test) is the direct cost of retrofitting rather than building for this from day one. Ozvor's methodology page is public, and every audit input is measured live — if a probe fails, the audit says so rather than filling the gap with a plausible number. For a prospect already paying $129-599/mo for Semrush or Ahrefs, our $29-99/mo entry tiers are additive rather than a rip-and-replace decision, and we're the only option in this comparison that turns the audit into content, not just a dashboard.

**When we lose**: A prospect deeply embedded in Semrush or Ahrefs for traditional SEO, who wants "one more tab" in a tool they already use daily and has no interest in AI-specific action plans or content drafting, will reasonably default to the incumbent's bolt-on rather than adopt a new vendor — inertia and existing workflow integration are real advantages here.

**Landmine question**: "Have you compared what Brand Radar reports for your ChatGPT mentions against what you find manually searching the same prompts yourself?"

## Universal Positioning — 5 Bullets

- **We are the only one in this set that closes the loop.** Every competitor here stops at a dashboard; Ozvor turns the audit into a plan and the plan into content drafts in the same product, with a DFY arm (OrganicPosts) for teams that want it built for them.
- **Radical measurement honesty is our wedge, not a slogan.** Our methodology is public and audits fail honestly rather than invent a number — a direct answer to accuracy concerns raised independently about at least one bolt-on competitor (Ahrefs Brand Radar, per third-party testing cited above).
- **We are priced for the buyer this whole category ignores.** Every competitor's "real" tier — Profound's $399/mo Growth, AthenaHQ's $295/mo Starter, Ahrefs' ~$828/mo full-coverage stack — prices out solo founders and small agencies. Our $0 → $29 → $99 → $549 ladder does not.
- **BYOK content generation is unique among the five.** No competitor reviewed publicly discloses a client-controlled model/cost mechanism for content generation; most don't generate content at all.
- **We are not claiming to out-monitor the specialists.** Peec (5.0 G2, 9 reviews) and Profound (4.6 G2, 845 reviews) have real monitoring depth and enterprise trust signals we do not yet have — our pitch is completeness and accessibility, not raw engine-count superiority.
