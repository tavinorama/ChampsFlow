## Channel Strategy

### LinkedIn — 1 founder post per week
Format: 3–4 sentence insight or story. First line is the hook — no "excited to announce" openers. Link to the week's blog post in the first comment (not in the body) to protect reach. Founder account preferred over brand account for pre-launch — higher organic reach, more credible for ICP Segment A (time-starved SMB owner). If founder opts out (open decision D4): publish from brand account, third-person tone.

### X — 2–3 posts per week
Format: 1–3 sentences or short thread (3–5 tweets). Mix: one insight post (no link), one link post to blog, one engagement post (question or observation). Build-in-public narrative is appropriate for X — progress updates, waitlist milestone posts, design decisions are honest and resonant with Indie Hackers-adjacent audience.

### Blog — 1 post per week
Format: 1200–2500 words. Target one specific keyword per post. Includes: intro, 3–5 H2 sections, FAQ block (for FAQPage JSON-LD schema), internal links to other planned content, waitlist CTA at end. JSON-LD Article schema on every post. Submitted to Google Search Console for indexing immediately on publish.

### Communities — as noted per piece
Updated target communities: Indie Hackers (founder-to-founder, build-in-public), r/smallbusiness (ICP Segment A, problem-statement pieces only — no overt promotion), SMB Twitter/X circle, /r/ChatGPT (GEO angle pieces — share as an insight observation, not a link drop), /r/ArtificialIntelligence (GEO angle pieces — same rules). Community posts must follow each platform's rules: no spam, no repeated link-dropping, lead with value.
