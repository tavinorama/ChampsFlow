## 2 · Your top 3 get-cited fixes

Prioritized so you start with the highest impact for the least effort.

### Fix 1 — Let the AI crawlers in *(effort: low · impact: high)*

If your `robots.txt` blocks the AI crawlers, engines can't read your site, so they can't cite it — no matter how good your content is. Add these lines:

```
User-agent: GPTBot
Allow: /

User-agent: ClaudeBot
Allow: /

User-agent: PerplexityBot
Allow: /

User-agent: Google-Extended
Allow: /
```

This is the single most common reason a good business is invisible to AI. It takes five minutes and it unblocks everything else.

### Fix 2 — Give AI structured data to read *(effort: medium · impact: high)*

AI engines parse structured data (schema.org) far more reliably than prose. Add three types to your key pages:

- **Organization** on your homepage — who you are, so the engine can treat you as a real entity.
- **FAQPage** on pages that answer buyer questions — this is the format AI Overviews pull from most.
- **Article** on your blog posts — so your content is machine-readable, not just human-readable.

You don't need a developer for the basics: most site builders have a schema field or plugin, and your three drafts below already include the markup.

### Fix 3 — Show up where AI already looks *(effort: medium · impact: high)*

AI engines cite third-party sources constantly — and the same few come up again and again: **Reddit, LinkedIn, and review sites like G2**. If your business is absent there, you are absent from a large share of the answers. Start with one genuine, helpful presence on the platform your buyers actually use. You don't need all three this month; you need to stop being invisible on the one that matters most.

## 3 · Three ready-to-publish drafts

Each draft is written to close one of the fixes above and to be the kind of content AI cites: a clear question, a direct answer, and the structured markup engines read. Review, adjust to your voice, and publish.

### Draft A — A blog post that answers a buyer question

**Format:** a "how does X work / which is best for Y" post — the exact shape of question AI engines get asked and pull answers from.

**Structure to follow:**
- **Title:** phrase it as the question a buyer would type ("How do I choose a [your category] for [their situation]?").
- **Overview:** two or three sentences that frame the problem plainly.
- **The answer:** your genuine, specific take — the more concrete, the more citable.
- **Why it matters:** one data point or example that makes it credible.
- **Add FAQPage schema** with the question and your answer.

### Draft B — A comparison page

**Format:** an honest "[you] vs [alternative]" page. Buyers ask AI to compare options constantly; if you don't publish the comparison, someone else's version is what AI repeats.

**Structure to follow:**
- A short, fair table of the real differences.
- One paragraph on who each option is genuinely best for — including where you're *not* the answer. That honesty is what makes the page trustworthy enough to cite.
- **Add Article + FAQPage schema.**

### Draft C — A LinkedIn post that builds your authority signal

**Format:** a proof-of-work post that links back to the content above. This is how you start showing up on the third-party sources from Fix 3.

**Structure to follow:**
- Open with the specific problem your buyer has.
- Share one real insight or result — concrete beats clever.
- Link to your new page. Consistency here compounds: it is the flywheel behind Citation Readiness.

## 4 · Your 30-day retest plan

Improvement in AI search is not instant and it is not linear. Here is the rhythm that works.

| Week | Do this | Then |
|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** | Ship Fix 1 (crawlers) and publish Draft A. | The fastest wins — crawler access unblocks everything. |
| **Week 2** | Add schema (Fix 2) to your key pages; publish Draft B. | Citation Readiness starts to move first — engines can now read you. |
| **Week 3** | Start Fix 3 — one genuine presence on Reddit, LinkedIn or a review site. Publish Draft C. | Off-site authority is slower but it's the biggest long-term lever. |
| **Week 4** | Re-run your audit and compare, question by question. | Some weeks show little movement, then a jump. Patience is part of the method. |

**What "working" looks like:** more of your buyer questions naming you, and Citation Readiness climbing first (it responds fastest to the technical fixes). Track it — what you don't measure, you can't improve.
