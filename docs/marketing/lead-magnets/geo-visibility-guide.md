## How AI engines actually decide who to cite

There's a myth that a magic tag or file makes ChatGPT recommend you. It doesn't. **AI engines cite the clearest, most credible, most current answer to a question — and that comes from content, not code.**

**Two doors, two speeds.**
- **Training memory** (slow): what the model baked in when it was built. This is why Wikipedia and big publishers surface constantly. Anything you publish today won't reach this door until the next training run — months to a year away.
- **Live retrieval** (fast): the AI searches the web mid-answer and cites what it finds. A page you publish now can be cited within days or weeks. But ChatGPT only runs a live search on an estimated ~34.5% of queries (Siana Marketing, 2026, directional) — the rest answer from memory alone. You need both doors open.

**Matching is by meaning, not keywords.** "We're passionate about beautiful smiles" matches nothing — no customer types that. "Invisalign in Austin runs $3,500–$6,000 and takes 12–18 months" matches a real question with a number, place, and timeframe.

| Engine | How it retrieves | What it means for you |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT | Live search on ~1 in 3 queries; heavy on Bing, Wikipedia, Reddit | Be indexed on Bing and answer-ready; don't rely on it always searching |
| Perplexity | Retrieval-first — nearly every answer is a live search | Your fastest feedback loop; fresh, accessible pages surface soonest |
| Claude | Searches live only when the question needs current info | Reputation and sourcing across the wider web matter most |
| Gemini / Google AIO | Tied to Google's live index; most reliable engine for reading schema | Classic SEO crawlability still carries real weight |

**Skip the "magic markup."** In a synthesis of 54 experiments, structured data (schema) scored just 5.6 out of 10 on citation impact — well below content and crawlability (Cyrus Shepard / Zyppy, 2026). `llms.txt` scored 2.0, near the bottom, with no credible evidence it earns citations (same source). Schema is fine basic hygiene; it is not the lever. If a vendor's pitch is "add our tag and ChatGPT will recommend you," that's not what the evidence shows.

Winning on one engine doesn't mean winning on the next: only 45% of the top-20 traditional local-search brands also appear in the top-20 AI recommendations (SOCi 2026). That's why checking across all five engines — not guessing at one — matters.

## The 5 traits that get content cited

In 2024, researchers from Princeton, Georgia Tech, the Allen Institute for AI, and IIT Delhi ran a controlled experiment — 10,000 real queries across 25 domains — testing nine ways of editing content to see what actually earned citations. [Aggarwal et al., *GEO: Generative Engine Optimization*, KDD 2024, arXiv:2311.09735] The result: the right edits lifted a page's AI visibility by up to 40%. The moves that worked are the hallmarks of genuinely credible writing — not tricks.

| # | Trait | Visibility lift | Do this |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Quotations** from credible sources | **+41%** (strongest single tactic) | Find one quotable line from an expert, regulator, or study and build a short post around it, sourced by name. |
| 2 | **Statistics** — concrete numbers | **+33%** | Pull a real number from your own work ("across our last 20 jobs, average X") and use it, labeled honestly as your own data. |
| 3 | **Citing sources** inline | **+28%** avg, **up to +115%** for an underdog page ranked 5th | Add two or three named, checkable citations to your best existing post. |
| 4 | **Specificity** — answer one narrow question exactly | Matches the way AI retrieval works (meaning-matching) | Write down the three most specific questions customers ask on a first call. Answer just one, completely. |
| 5 | **Single-idea focus + a clear stance**, published on a steady cadence | Hedged, everything-at-once posts get cited for nothing | Pick two fixed publishing days a week. One idea per post, with a number or source in every one. |

**The one thing that backfires:** keyword stuffing — the old SEO reflex — cut visibility by **−8.7%**, the only tactic in the study that hurt. AI engines parse meaning, not keyword density.

> **Underdog example (financial advisor).** BAD: "Index funds are usually a smart choice for long-term investors." GOOD: "Per S&P Dow Jones Indices' SPIVA scorecard, ~88% of large-cap U.S. fund managers underperformed the S&P 500 over the 15 years ending 2023 — that's the case for low-cost index funds in one sentence." The second version anchors the claim to a named, checkable source, and per the research, a page like this starting at position 5 can more than double its visibility.

You don't need a big following to benefit from any of this — Semrush found the *median* AI-cited LinkedIn post had just 15–25 reactions (Jan–Feb 2026). The model isn't counting your likes. It's reading your answer.

## Where AI actually looks

A handful of domains account for most AI citations — which tells you where an hour of effort buys the most visibility.

| Rank | Source | Share of citations | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reddit | ~3.1% of all citations; #1 on Perplexity | Post there today, for free (Profound, 4B+ citations) |
| 2 | LinkedIn | ~11% avg citation rate, up to 14.3% on ChatGPT Search | Original posts, not follower count, drive this (Semrush) |
| 3 | Wikipedia | 16.3% of ChatGPT's citations | Largely out of reach for a business page |
| 4 | YouTube | ~2.1% overall | Reviews and demos |
| — | Quora | 4th most-cited in Google's AI Mode (~7.25%) | Long, complete answers (535+ words) win here |

**The honest catch:** Ahrefs found 67.7% of ChatGPT's top 1,000 citations are effectively off-limits to marketers — Wikipedia, brand homepages, app stores. **Only ~32.3% of citation "seats" are genuinely contestable**: educational pages, review sites, news, and blogs (Ahrefs, Sep 2025). That's clarifying, not discouraging — it tells you exactly where to spend effort.

**Your fastest, most controllable win: reviews.** Vendor research indicates brands on 2+ review platforms are roughly 3.4x more likely to be mentioned by ChatGPT (G2 / SE Ranking roundup, directional). For local businesses, SOCi found AI-recommended businesses averaged 4.3 stars. Get listed and reviewed on at least two relevant platforms this week.

| Your business | Lead with | Then |
|---|---|---|
| Local services (HVAC, plumbing, dental, restaurants) | Google Business Profile + reviews | Reddit (city/niche subreddits) |
| B2B software / SaaS | G2 + Capterra reviews | LinkedIn posts answering buyer questions |
| Professional services (law, accounting, consulting) | LinkedIn original posts | Quora answers in your specialty |
| Local retail / food | Google Business Profile, flawless and complete | Steady review requests |

One more truth: where AI pulls from **shifts monthly** — Reddit's share of ChatGPT citations swung from ~60% to ~10% in six weeks in 2025 (Semrush). That volatility is the argument for ongoing monitoring, not a one-time audit.

## Your 4-week posting plan

Eight posts, two a week, fifteen minutes of research before each one. Built on the Princeton findings above and on Semrush's analysis of which LinkedIn posts AI engines actually cite.

**Before every post (15 minutes):** collect one of — a statistic with a named source and date, a quotable line you can attribute, or a specific observation from your own work this week ("across our last 20 installs, the average..."). **Never publish a post with none of the three.**

| Week | Monday | Thursday |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Data Story** — one number, the source, what it means for your customer (150–250 words) | **The How-We-Did-It Case** — situation → what you did → result, one real number (150–250 words) |
| 2 | **The Contrarian Take** — one piece of common advice that's wrong for your customer, and why (200–300 words) | **The Full FAQ Answer** — your most-asked question, answered completely, no teaser (200–300 words) |
| 3 | **The Single-Niche Post** — speak to one specific customer type, not "everyone" (150–250 words) | **The Mistake Confession** — a real mistake and the correct approach (150–250 words) |
| 4 | **The Definition Frame** — define one loosely-used industry term precisely for your customer (150–250 words) | **The Review** — one concrete thing you observed this month; set up week 5 (100–200 words) |

**Distribution order:** LinkedIn first, always (its ~11% average citation rate makes it the highest-probability surface) → Facebook as written → Instagram as a caption extract → your own site/blog last, for the compounding, freshness-favored copy.

**Pre-publish checklist — every post, every time:**
- [ ] One specific anchor: a number, a named source, or a real described situation
- [ ] Answers one question completely, not seven half-answers
- [ ] Phrased the way a customer would actually ask
- [ ] A clear, quotable takeaway
- [ ] Under 20% "book/buy/call us" — AI cites answers, not pitches

## Local & industry specifics

For "near me" businesses, the shift is starkest: ChatGPT recommends only ~1.2% of locations, versus 35.9% that appear in Google's local 3-pack (SOCi 2026). Winning old local SEO doesn't carry over — only 45% of top-20 traditional local brands also rank top-20 in AI. And AI is quietly wrong more often than you'd expect: business-profile details were only ~68% accurate on ChatGPT and Perplexity (SOCi 2026) — your hours, address, and services, described with a 1-in-3 chance of being off.

**Three levers that move local AI visibility:**

1. **Reviews, in volume and recency.** Vendor research suggests 50+ recent reviews correlate with ~3x higher odds of AI recommendation (GrowthPro, directional). Build a same-day text-with-link habit rather than a one-time push.
2. **An accurate Google Business Profile.** Fill every field — services, service areas, hours, plain-language descriptions ("emergency water heater repair," not "residential thermal solutions"). Gemini scored 100% profile accuracy pulling straight from it (SOCi 2026).
3. **Recency everywhere.** AI-cited pages run ~25.7% fresher than top-10 organic results (Ahrefs, ~17M citations, Dec 2025). A regularly updated FAQ or service page beats a static one.

**One honest caveat:** conversion-lift claims vary by study. Amsive's 54-site study found AI and organic conversion statistically indistinguishable overall (4.87% vs. 4.60%, p=0.794), even as some other studies report higher lifts. We'd rather you see the range than a cherry-picked number.

## Six mistakes that quietly cost you citations

| Mistake | Why it fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Over-promotion | The Princeton study found keyword-stuffed, sales-first content is the *only* tactic that reduced visibility (−8.7%) | Keep pitches under 20% of any post |
| Jargon without explanation | Retrieval matches customers' actual words, not industry terms | Write "why is my AC freezing up," not "evaporator coil ice formation" |
| Daily-posting fatigue | Volume without quality doesn't help — the median cited post had 15–25 reactions, not virality | Two sharp posts a week beats fourteen rushed ones |
| Engagement bait ("drop a 🔥") | Built for platform reach, invisible to AI retrieval | Write to answer the question, not farm comments |
| Inconsistency | Freshness is a relevance signal that compounds; gaps forfeit your seat to a competitor | Hold the two-day-a-week cadence, every week |
| Keyword stuffing | Measured at −8.7% visibility — the only tactic that backfired | Say the thing once, clearly, in context |

## How to measure what's working

There's no official API that reports when an AI names your business — measurement is hands-on, and entirely doable.

1. Pick 8–10 real-prospect queries ("best [service] in [city]," "[service] vs [alternative]," "how much does [service] cost").
2. Run each one weekly across all four engines.
3. Log: were you named? Quoted or paraphrased? What source did the engine link? Was the description accurate?
4. Track it in a simple spreadsheet, or let Ozvor automate the sweep and roll it into one score.

| Timeframe | What to expect |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1–3 | No citations yet — normal, not failure. You're building the base. |
| ~Week 4 | First movement on narrow, specific queries |
| Weeks 4–8 | Citations accumulate; descriptions start firming up |
| 3+ months | Broader, higher-competition queries begin to move |

**Reading your results:** named and accurate → keep the cadence, expand to adjacent queries. Named but wrong → strengthen your owned profiles so the engine has accurate material to pull from. Not named after 8 weeks of consistent posting → your content likely still doesn't match how customers phrase questions, or you're in a low-volume niche.

Why it's worth the effort: Seer Interactive found brands cited inside a Google AI Overview earn ~120% more clicks on the same query, and Microsoft Clarity found AI-referred visitors sign up at markedly higher rates than search visitors — though, again, Amsive's 54-site study found no significant conversion difference overall. The direction is consistently positive; treat the exact multiplier as directional.
